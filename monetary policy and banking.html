Monetary Policy and Banking

Course Overview

Monetary Policy and Banking is a course that explores the role of central banks in managing the economy through monetary policy.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the basic concepts and principles of monetary policy.
  • Analyze the role of central banks in managing the economy.
  • Evaluate the impact of monetary policy on inflation, output, and employment.
  • Apply theoretical concepts to real-world problems in monetary policy and banking.
  • Think critically about the challenges and limitations of monetary policy.

Course Topics

  1. Introduction to Monetary Policy: Definition, scope, and importance of monetary policy.
  2. Central Banking: Organization, functions, and objectives of central banks.
  3. Monetary Policy Tools: Open market operations, reserve requirements, and interest rates.
  4. Monetary Policy Transmission Mechanism: How monetary policy affects the economy.
  5. Inflation Targeting: The role of inflation targeting in monetary policy.
  6. Financial Stability: The role of central banks in maintaining financial stability.
  7. Banking Regulation and Supervision: The role of central banks in regulating and supervising banks.
  8. Monetary Policy in Practice: Case studies of monetary policy in different countries.

Learning Outcomes

  • Theoretical foundations of monetary policy.
  • Role of central banks in managing the economy.
  • Impact of monetary policy on inflation, output, and employment.
  • Monetary policy tools and transmission mechanism.
  • Critical thinking and problem-solving skills in monetary policy and banking.

Assessment

  • Quizzes and exams.
  • Homework assignments and problem sets.
  • Group projects and presentations.
  • Final research paper or project.

Prerequisites

None.

Target Audience

  • Undergraduate students in economics, finance, or related fields.
  • Graduate students in economics, finance, or related fields.
  • Professionals seeking to understand monetary policy and banking.

Recommended Textbooks

  • Mishkin, F. S. (2020). The Economics of Money, Banking, and Financial Markets.
  • Kroszner, R. S., & Shiller, R. J. (2020). Reforming U.S. Financial Markets.
  • Goodfriend, M. (2020). Monetary Policy and the Economy.

Software Requirements

None.
