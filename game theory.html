Game Theory

Course Overview

Game Theory is a course that explores the strategic decision-making process in situations where the outcome depends on the actions of multiple individuals or parties.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the basic concepts and principles of game theory.
  • Analyze strategic situations and predict the behavior of players.
  • Apply game-theoretic concepts to real-world problems in economics, politics, and social sciences.
  • Think critically about the strategic interactions between individuals and groups.
  • Develop problem-solving skills using game-theoretic tools and techniques.

Course Topics

  1. Introduction to Game Theory: Definition, scope, and importance of game theory.
  2. Strategic Thinking: Basic concepts of strategic thinking, including rationality, uncertainty, and risk.
  3. Nash Equilibrium: Definition and application of Nash equilibrium.
  4. Pareto Optimality: Definition and application of Pareto optimality.
  5. Game Trees and Strategic Decision-Making: Analysis of game trees and strategic decision-making.
  6. Auctions and Bargaining: Analysis of auctions and bargaining games.
  7. Cooperative Game Theory: Introduction to cooperative game theory and its application.
  8. Game Theory and Real-World Applications: Application of game theory to real-world problems in economics, politics, and social sciences.

Learning Outcomes

  • Theoretical foundations of game theory.
  • Strategic thinking and decision-making.
  • Nash equilibrium and Pareto optimality.
  • Game-theoretic concepts and their application to real-world problems.
  • Critical thinking and problem-solving skills using game-theoretic tools and techniques.

Assessment

  • Quizzes and exams.
  • Homework assignments and problem sets.
  • Group projects and presentations.
  • Final research paper or project.

Prerequisites

None.

Target Audience

  • Undergraduate students in economics, mathematics, computer science, or related fields.
  • Graduate students in economics, mathematics, computer science, or related fields.
  • Professionals seeking to understand game theory and its application to real-world problems.

Recommended Textbooks

  • Myerson, R. B. (2020). Game Theory: Analysis of Conflict.
  • Fudenberg, D., & Tirole, J. (2020). Game Theory.
  • Osborne, M. J., & Rubinstein, A. (2020). A Course in Game Theory.

Software Requirements

None.
