Welfare Economics

Course Overview

Welfare Economics is a course that explores the economic principles and concepts that underlie the evaluation of economic policies and their impact on social welfare.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the basic concepts and principles of welfare economics.
  • Analyze the impact of economic policies on social welfare.
  • Evaluate the efficiency and equity implications of different economic policies.
  • Apply cost-benefit analysis to evaluate the desirability of different economic projects.
  • Think critically about the limitations and challenges of applying welfare economics in real-world contexts.

Course Topics

  1. Introduction to Welfare Economics: Definition, scope, and importance of welfare economics.
  2. Consumer Surplus and Producer Surplus: Concepts and measurement of consumer surplus and producer surplus.
  3. Social Welfare Functions: Definition and types of social welfare functions.
  4. Cost-Benefit Analysis: Principles and application of cost-benefit analysis.
  5. Market Failure and Externalities: Causes and consequences of market failure and externalities.
  6. Public Goods and Common Resources: Characteristics and provision of public goods and common resources.
  7. Welfare Economics and Policy: Application of welfare economics to policy issues.

Learning Outcomes

  • Theoretical foundations of welfare economics.
  • Concepts and measurement of consumer surplus and producer surplus.
  • Social welfare functions and their application.
  • Principles and application of cost-benefit analysis.
  • Implications of market failure and externalities for social welfare.

Assessment

  • Quizzes and exams.
  • Homework assignments and problem sets.
  • Group projects and presentations.
  • Final research paper or project.

Prerequisites

None.

Target Audience

  • Undergraduate students in economics, public policy, or related fields.
  • Graduate students in economics, public policy, or related fields.
  • Professionals seeking to understand welfare economics and its application to policy issues.

Recommended Textbooks

  • Just, R. E., Hueth, D. L., & Schmitz, A. (2020). Applied Welfare Economics.
  • Boadway, R. W., & Bruce, N. (2020). Welfare Economics.
  • Mas-Colell, A., Whinston, M. D., & Green, J. R. (2020). Microeconomic Theory.

Software Requirements

None.
