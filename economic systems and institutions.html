Economic Systems and Institutions

Course Overview

Economic Systems and Institutions is a course that explores the different economic systems and institutions that exist around the world.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the basic concepts and principles of economic systems.
  • Analyze the characteristics and functioning of different economic systems.
  • Evaluate the strengths and weaknesses of different economic systems.
  • Apply knowledge of economic systems to real-world problems.
  • Think critically about the role of institutions in shaping economic outcomes.

Course Topics

  1. Introduction to Economic Systems: Definition, scope, and importance of economic systems.
  2. Traditional Economic Systems: Characteristics and functioning of traditional economies.
  3. Command Economic Systems: Characteristics and functioning of command economies.
  4. Market Economic Systems: Characteristics and functioning of market economies.
  5. Mixed Economic Systems: Characteristics and functioning of mixed economies.
  6. Institutions and Economic Outcomes: Role of institutions in shaping economic outcomes.
  7. Comparative Economic Systems: Comparison of different economic systems.

Learning Outcomes

  • Theoretical foundations of economic systems.
  • Characteristics and functioning of different economic systems.
  • Role of institutions in shaping economic outcomes.
  • Comparative analysis of different economic systems.
  • Applications of economic systems to real-world problems.

Assessment

  • Quizzes and exams.
  • Homework assignments and problem sets.
  • Group projects and presentations.
  • Final research paper or project.

Prerequisites

None.

Target Audience

  • Undergraduate students in economics, business, or related fields.
  • Graduate students in economics, business, or related fields.
  • Professionals seeking to understand economic systems and institutions.

Recommended Textbooks

  • Gregory, P. R., & Stuart, R. C. (2020). Comparative Economic Systems.
  • Rosser, M. V., & Rosser, J. B. (2020). Comparative Economics in a Transforming World.
  • Schneider, F. (2020). The Economics of Transition.

Software Requirements

None.
