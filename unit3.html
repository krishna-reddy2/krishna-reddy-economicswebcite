meaning of production

  1. Production means creation of utilities
  2. All the goods and services which are helping to produce some other goods are called factors of production
  3. All the goods and services which are helping to produce some other goods are called factors of production
    • land
    • labour
    • capital
    • organisation

    4. In the ordinary language land means surface of the earth. But in economics land means not only the surface of the earth. It includes all natural resources.

    5. In the ordinary language labour means physical efforts of a worker. But in economics either physical work or mental work which is done for reward (or) remuneration is called labour.

    6. In the ordinary language capital means money. But in economics produce means of production is called capital.

    7. To co-ordinate the above 3 factors of production (land, labour, capital) one man is required. He is called organiser and his work is called organisation.

    8. Factors of production are also known as inputs.
